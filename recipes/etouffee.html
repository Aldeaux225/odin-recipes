Etouffee

And last but not least, it's time to step away from the meats and grab some seafood.

When people think of Louisiana, they think of two things: Swamps and Crawfish, and what better way to prove them right than serving up a plate of home-made crawfish etouffee. Perfect as a side or just a meal by itself, this dish will have you coming back for seconds and maybe even a midnight snack (just don't forget the tums).

Ingredients:

  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Sticks of Celery
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper
  • 1 Bunch of Green Onions
  • 4 Cloves of Garlic
  • 2 Sticks of Butter
  • 1 Tablespoon Cajun or Creole Seasoning
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Cayenne Pepper
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1/3 Cup of Flour
  • 2 lbs Louisiana Crawfish
  • 2 Cups of Hot Water (Add more if desired)

Steps:

  1. Start out by heating a Pot or Large Pan to a Medium/Low heat.
  2. Chop the Onion, Celery, Red Bell Pepper, Green Bell Pepper, and Green Onions.
  3. Drop two Sticks of Butter into your hot pot and melt completely.
  4. Add the chopped vegetables to the pot and raise burner to a medium.
  5. Add the Cajun Seasoning, Cayenne Pepper, and Salt.
  6. Sauté for about 20 minutes.
  7. Meanwhile chop the four cloves of Garlic.
  8. After 20 minuets of sautéing, add the garlic and sauté for another 10 minutes.
  9. After 10 more minuets of sautéing, push the vegetables to one side of the pot, add the 1/3 cup of flour and blend into the butter. Once the flour is mixed in well, stir everything altogether.
  10. Sauté for another 30 minutes. Be very cautious during this time. If fire seems a little high, lower it.
  11. Around this time you want to begin cooking some rice.
  12. With about 3 minutes left of sautéing, begin heating 2 cups of water in the microwave.
  13. Add a little bit of the hot water to the pot, and stir until it’s a creamy mixture.
  14. Add the Crawfish and rest of the hot water to the pot. Blend evenly.
  15. Raise the heat to where you see a slight boil.
  16. Cover, and lower to a simmering heat. Cook for about 15-20 minutes.
  17. (f you feel as though you would like a thinner sauce, just add a little more water. However, taste to see if you need more seasoning.)
